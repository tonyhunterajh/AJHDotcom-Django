Pitmaster Background

In 1996 I went to St. Louis for a day trip. On the way home I was hungry so I stopped at a BBQ joint called Bandana's BBQ. In Indianapolis, BBQ typically comes in the form of high-heat grilled ribs and rib tips so, I was expecting more of the same here. On the table in the restaurant was a pamphlet explaining why the ribs would be pink around the edges, and informing me there was no need for concern - the ribs were cooked to doneness and the pink around the edges is nothing more than a "smokering". I had never heard of any smokering before, so I was cautiously interested in seeing what these ribs would look like and taste like.

When the food arrived, it looked and smelled insanely delicious! In fact, the entire restaurant smelled delicious... like smoke wood. The ribs indeed had a pink ring around the edges but my goodness, did they taste amazing! They had a smokey flavor and were so tender! These ribs were so good I bought to slabs to go! I wanted my family and friends to taste these ribs.

Immediately when I got home to Indy, I started searching the web for smoked ribs, smokerings and southern BBQ. I found a wealth of information and learned so much about smoking meats. I bought a smoker from a local hardware store and jumped in the game with both feet.

Since then, I have competed in BBQ competitions, cooked for numerous events for family and friends, and I am now the owner and Pitmaster of Indy Smokehouse BBQ. My full-time jobs prevents me from cooking the number of events I would like to, but I still enjoy a loyal following of local patrons who want my cooking from time to time.

Goob barbecue don't need no sauce!

Pitmaster Tony Hunter
